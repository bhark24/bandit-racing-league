========================================================================
             BANDIT RACING LEAGUE - FRANCHISE TEAM ECONOMY EXPLANATION
========================================================================

The Bandit Racing League (BRL) economy dashboard simulates team-wide rosters,
finances, fleet truck maintenance, and active sponsorships. Below is a comprehensive
breakdown of how the team economy and rules operate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. FRANCHISE TEAMS & ROSTERS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Teams consist of 4 primary driver slots and 2 backup driver slots.
- Teams are not required to maintain a full lineup, and some may have vacant slots (never reaching 6 total drivers).
- Active Lineup Substitution (DNS Auto-Sub):
  - Prior to weekly scoring, the system checks the active drivers.
  - If a primary driver is absent (DNS), the system automatically scans the team's
    backup drivers and substitutes a backup driver who participated.
  - If no backup driver participated, the slot remains vacant and scores a DNS.

- Driver Signings & Releases:
  - Signing a new free agent driver requires owner passcode authorization and
    deducts a flat $50,000 signing bonus from team capital.
  - Releasing an active driver returns them to the free agency pool (cost: $0).

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. TRUCK FLEET & DAMAGE MECHANICS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Teams are required to purchase and maintain one Craftsman Truck for each of their active full-time (primary) drivers. For example, if a franchise only has 2 primary drivers, they only need to purchase and maintain 2 trucks (rather than a full fleet of 4). Trucks degrade based on wear and race incidents:

- Natural Wear: Deducts 2-5% condition per race.
- Incident Damage: Deducts 4% condition per incident in the race.
- Mechanical / Engine Failures:
  - If a driver DNF's due to a blown motor, the engine must be replaced at
    the end of the race for a flat fee of $45,000 (restores condition to 100%).
- Fast Repairs / Crash damage:
  - If a driver DNF's due to crash damage and used a Fast Repair, the truck is
    totaled. It must be replaced with a new truck for $185,000.
  - If a driver uses a Fast Repair but does NOT DNF, the team is charged a flat
    Fast Repair fee of $83,250 (restores condition to 100% immediately).
- Manual Chassis Repairs:
  - Owners can manually repair a truck at any time using the dashboard.
  - Repairs cost $500 per 1% condition restored (restores condition to 100%).
  - Repairs require verification of the owner's passcode.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. FINANCES & ECONOMY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Franchise balances are updated at the end of each simulation week:

Weekly Earnings:
- Sponsor Start Bonus: +$15,000 per starting driver.
- Position Prize Money:
  - P1: $75,000
  - P2: $55,000
  - P3: $45,000
  - P4: $35,000
  - P5: $30,000
  - P6 - P10: $25,000
  - P11 - P15: $20,000
  - P16 - P20: $15,000
  - P21+: $12,000
- Virtual Sponsorships:
  - Evaluated weekly based on the average finishing position of all participating drivers:
  - Upward Performance (Average finish of 12.0 or better): 50% chance of gaining a sponsor,
    yielding a random start payout of $10,000 - $35,000.
  - Downward Performance (Average finish of 20.0 or worse): 40% chance of losing a sponsor,
    costing a random capital loss of $5,000 - $20,000.

Weekly Expenses:
- Driver Travel & Lodging: Calculated based on driver's home city coordinates
  to the track coordinates:
  - Local commute (<= 50 mi): $20
  - Driving distance (51 - 250 mi): $100
  - Flight distance (> 250 mi): Seasonally adjusted flight cost ($250 base + $0.20/mi).
- Hauler Logistics: Cost to transport the truck fleet from the team's home base
  to the track coordinates, calculated at $5.00/mi round-trip.
- Quarterly Hauler Maintenance: Charged $2,000 every 4 simulation races.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. EMERGENCY LOAN & ACTION LOCK SYSTEM
------------------------------------------------------------------------
To manage financial distress when expenses exceed a team's cash reserves:

- Emergency Loan Issuance:
  - If a team's weekly balance drops below zero, a loan is automatically issued
    to cover the debt, bringing the team's cash balance back to exactly $0.
  - The loan balance increases by the absolute value of the debt.
  
- Automated Repayment:
  - While a team has an active loan balance (loan > 0), 50% of all future weekly
    earnings (sponsor start bonuses, prize money, sponsor payouts) is automatically
    deducted and applied to repay the loan before updating the cash balance.

- Loan Interest:
  - Any remaining active loan balance accumulates 2% weekly interest at the end
    of each simulation week.

- Financial Action Lock:
  - While a team has an active loan balance (loan > 0), they are locked out of
    manual franchise actions. Specifically, they cannot:
    1. Repair fleet trucks.
    2. Sign new free agent drivers.
  - These actions are disabled in the UI (buttons shown as locked/disabled) and
    blocked in the command-line/backend scripts.
